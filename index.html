What you will learn

April's conference will answer your most critical questions about Scaling up your food or drinks startup, including:

  • How Can I Make the Buyer Want to Stock My Product?
  • How can I get my products into the stores that matter?
  • When you should turn down a supermarket’s offer to stock you

  • What are the most effective marketing strategies to ensure continuous sales?














Hello!

Hi, I'm Victoria, a German girl that loves food & tech.
I came to London in 2014, having previously co-founded and run a Food Festival in Scotland for 3 years. Upon my arrival in the Big Smoke I did some research to seek out the food startup scene and connect with food and food tech entrepreneurs but I quickly realised that the community and events I was hoping for didn't exist. I spoke to about 150 food startups in London over the course of several months and realised that there was a real need and opportunity to create an event and brand that brought entrepreneurs together and would help them take their startup to the next level. You can have a read about my journey here and here.


Making it big

I want to give food startups more opportunities to make it big. We eat so much bad food every single day because it's all that mainstream supermarkets will stock. Interesting products are difficult to discover because many of them don't reach the scale where they could be found in bigger stores. I want empower entrepreneurs with the skills, the network and the inspiration to take their business to the next level.


What's Special About Food StartUp School

With a few incredible entrepreneurs and Food StartUp School supporters by my side, our events bring together passionnate food and drinks entrepreneurs from London to give attendees the opportunity to gain valuable insights form the successes and sometimes also failures of fellow food entrepreneurs. Workshops provide concentrated learning, talks offer observations of similar journeys and open for discussion afterwards, and networking (plenty of it) creates the most effective way to bring the industry closer together by encouraging a collaborative environment. We're working with some incredible international partners and sponsors this year, and I hope you're as excited as I am.

Special Guest

In-Depth Knowledge, Expertise Tipps and Tools From Entrepreneurs and Industry Experts
Melissa Addey - How To Make The Buyer Call You

Melissa worked for Sainsbury's Head Office for five years, both as a buyer and as an Innovation Project Manager developing new products and packaging as well as managing the organic range. During her time managing organic food at Sainsbury’s the range won over 20 industry, consumer and media awards including Organic Supermarket of the Year (twice) and went from £170m to £240m turnover.

For the past six years Melissa has worked on various government business support programmes, supporting small and medium businesses through mentoring, workshops, events, facilitating collaboration with universities as well as consultants and grant-supported projects. She is now working on a book to be published at the end of April. Her talk will empower you to evaluate your product with a purchaser's mind.


Consulting and Advisory work successes of Melissa include:

  • Café tripled turnover
  • Pastry manufacturer reduced food waste by 200 tonnes
  • Sauce made by a Ghanaian restaurant won Gold Great Taste Award
  • Health drinks and sweetners getting into major retailers
  • Fast-food business developing eco-friendly takeaway containers

Buy Tickets to the Food StartUp School now!

The UK food, grocery and drink sector is currently worth £170bn and set to grow in value by 21% to nearly £206bn by 2018. We’ve all played with the thought of starting a food-related business – but it takes hard work and determination to make a career out of a food startup in this competitive industry.
How far are you ready to take your passion for the sweet taste of success? Many have already taken the leap, and whether straight from University or after several years into a corporate career, we have yet to meet someone would give up the freedom of being their own boss and growing the brand for a product they love. But with such a high early fail rate and a competitive landscape, what makes a food startup survive and succeed?

Whether you’re new to Food Startups and playing with the idea of starting one or have set sail on the Entrepreneur-Ship and are looking for ideas, skills or the right contacts to push your Food Startup to the next level, this conference will address your biggest challenges and connect you with relevant experts and peers.
